The Situation

A brand without a system

Tag1 Consulting is a global Drupal development agency with 100+ team members across 20+ countries. They're known for solving complex technical challenges — performance, scalability, infrastructure — but their marketing materials didn't reflect that expertise.

They had recently redesigned their brand identity, but lacked internal design resources to extend it consistently across the formats they needed: conference materials, marketing collateral, web pages, and sales support.

The Challenge

How do you take a newly designed brand and make it work across every format a global agency needs — from ebooks to conference booths to elevator clings — without a full-time designer on staff?

The Scope

Eight formats, one designer

I embedded with Tag1 as their design partner, delivering brand extensions across print, digital, and environmental formats. Here's what I created:

Takeaways

What I learned

This project reinforced the value of embedded design partnerships for agencies. They don't need a full-time designer — they need someone who can jump between formats, deliver fast, and maintain brand consistency without constant oversight.

Lesson 01

Brand consistency doesn't come from rigid rules — it comes from understanding the core visual language well enough to adapt it across wildly different formats.

Lesson 02

Dev agencies need design partners who can context-switch quickly. One day it's an ebook, the next it's a conference booth. That versatility is the value.
